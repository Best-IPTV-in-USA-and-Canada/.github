## What to Look for in a USA & Canada IPTV Service

**US and Canadian channels included** — Any serious service must carry the major broadcast networks: ABC, CBS, NBC, Fox, PBS, CTV, CBC, Global, and City TV at a minimum.

**Sports coverage** — This is the primary reason most Americans and Canadians switch. Look for NFL (all games, no blackouts), NBA, NHL, MLB, UFC/PPV, NASCAR, and college sports via ESPN, TNT, TNT Sports, TSN, and Sportsnet.

**Peak-hour performance** — Many providers choke during Sunday NFL games or UFC pay-per-view events. The best services use multi-region server architecture with load balancing so your stream never freezes when everyone else is watching the same game.

**4K and HD quality** — Look for H.265/HEVC compression and Adaptive Bitrate Streaming (ABR). ABR automatically adjusts quality if your connection dips, then jumps back to 4K once it stabilizes — no buffering, no manual adjustment.

**Device compatibility** — Good services work on Amazon Firestick, Smart TV (Samsung, LG), Android TV, Apple TV, iPhone, Android phones, tablets, Windows, and Mac without extra hardware.

**Internet speed needed** — 10 Mbps for HD, 25 Mbps for 4K. Most US and Canadian broadband connections run well above this threshold.
